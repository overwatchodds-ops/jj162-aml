Threshold Transaction Report (TTR)
A TTR must be filed with AUSTRAC if a customer pays $10,000 or more in physical cash (banknotes or coins). Electronic bank transfers do not trigger a TTR.
Suspicious Matter Report (SMR)
⚠ Tipping-off prohibition — s.123 AML/CTF Act
It is a criminal offence to disclose that an SMR has been or may be filed. Do not tell the buyer, seller, conveyancer or any other party. Penalties include up to 2 years imprisonment.

How to Use Legal Reliance Manually
Legal reliance allows you to rely on the buyer's conveyancer's identity verification instead of doing it yourself. SimpleAML doesn't automate this yet — here's how to do it manually and stay compliant.
1
Record the conveyancer's details. Note the firm name, practitioner name, phone and email. Keep this on file.
2
Send a written request. Email the conveyancer asking them to confirm in writing that they have verified the buyer's identity. A reply email is sufficient — it doesn't need to be a formal letter.
3
Keep the written confirmation. Save the reply email or letter. This is your audit evidence — without it, reliance is not valid.
4
Still do PEP and sanctions screening yourself. Reliance only covers identity verification. You must independently screen the buyer for PEPs and sanctions. This obligation cannot be delegated.
5
If the conveyancer refuses or doesn't respond, switch to direct verification (Option A) and collect ID from the buyer yourself. Consider whether the refusal itself is a red flag warranting an SMR.
Deadline still applies. Using legal reliance doesn't extend your buyer CDD deadline. You must have the conveyancer's written confirmation before the deadline — not just by settlement.

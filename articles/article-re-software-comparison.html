AML Software for Real Estate Agents Australia — Honest Comparison

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 7 min read ✍️ SimpleAML

There are now several AML/CTF tools targeting Australian real estate agents ahead of 1 July 2026. This guide compares the main options honestly — including SimpleAML's own strengths and limitations — so you can choose what's right for your agency.

📋 Disclosure

This article is written by SimpleAML. We've tried to be fair and accurate about all tools — including our own gaps. Where we couldn't verify a competitor's exact pricing or features, we've said so. If anything here is out of date, let us know.

The tools we're comparing

Four tools have emerged as the main options for Australian real estate agents needing AML/CTF compliance before 1 July 2026. They sit at very different points on the price-vs-features spectrum, and they suit very different agency types.

  • SimpleAML — free browser-based compliance tool built for small agencies
  • First AML — established enterprise platform from NZ, now targeting Australian RE
  • TrustSoft — Australian trust accounting software with an AML module added
  • AML Assured — newer Australian-built RE-specific platform, early-bird pricing

The honest breakdown

SimpleAML
realestate.simpleaml.com.au — built for small agencies
Free
$0 — no subscription, no per-check fees, no account required

SimpleAML was built by an Australian CPA specifically for small real estate agencies and accountants who needed a no-cost way to get compliant before 1 July 2026. It covers the core compliance framework — risk assessment, AML/CTF program upload, staff vetting, training register, AUSTRAC enrolment tracking, and an ongoing listing register for CDD. It doesn't do everything a large agency might need, and it's honest about that.

What it does well
  • Completely free — no card, no login
  • Open in browser and start immediately
  • Free downloadable Word templates (Part A, Part B, Risk Assessment)
  • Data stays on your device — not in someone else's cloud
  • Designed specifically for the July 2026 deadline
  • Plain-English guidance built into every step
  • Generates a compliance evidence report
What it doesn't do (yet)
  • No electronic ID verification built in
  • No PEP or sanctions screening
  • No cloud sync — data lives in your browser
  • No SMS/email verification requests to clients
  • Not suited to high-volume agencies (50+ transactions/month)
Best for: Small to medium agencies (1–10 agents) who want a structured, free way to get compliant by 1 July 2026. If you're a principal-led agency doing under 50 transactions a month, SimpleAML covers everything you legally need to have in place. For ID verification, pair it with a standalone service like NameScan.
First AML
firstaml.com/au — enterprise platform, NZ origin
Paid — contact for pricing
Not published — annual platform fee + per-verification usage. Industry estimates: $3,000–$10,000+ pa depending on volume.

First AML is the most established player in the space, having processed millions of AML cases across New Zealand, Australia and the UK. It's a full end-to-end platform that embeds ID verification, PEP and sanctions screening, beneficial ownership checks, and risk assessment into deal workflows. The REIA CEO has publicly endorsed it. It's a serious enterprise product — which means enterprise setup time and enterprise cost.

What it does well
  • Full electronic ID verification (drivers licence, passport)
  • PEP and sanctions screening built in
  • Handles companies, SMSFs, and complex structures
  • SMS/email verification requests to clients
  • REIA-endorsed, strong track record overseas
  • Scales to large multi-branch agencies
Limitations
  • Significant cost — not viable for small agencies
  • Pricing not transparent — requires sales contact
  • Setup and onboarding takes time
  • Overseas-origin product adapted for Australia
  • Per-verification fees add up at volume
Best for: Medium to large agencies and franchise groups with dedicated compliance staff and budget. If you're doing 100+ transactions a month and need automated client-facing verification workflows, First AML is the market-leading option — but be prepared to pay for it.
TrustSoft
trustsoft.com.au — trust accounting + AML module
~$1,200 pa
~$1,200 pa ex GST for up to 5 users, low-risk customers. Higher tiers available.

TrustSoft is primarily a trust accounting platform — it manages client funds for real estate agents, auctioneers, conveyancers and dealers. AML/CTF compliance has been added as a module on top. If your agency already uses TrustSoft for trust accounting, adding the AML module makes sense. If you're coming to it purely for AML compliance, you're paying for a lot of trust accounting infrastructure you may not need.

What it does well
  • Affordable entry-level pricing (~$1,200 pa)
  • Electronic KYC — drivers licence, passport, PEP checks
  • Australian-built and supported
  • Good fit if you also need trust accounting
  • Risk assessments and draft policies included
Limitations
  • Not RE-specific — built for multiple sectors
  • Requires a trust account with a bank to use fully
  • AML is a module, not the core product
  • Less intuitive for agents unfamiliar with trust accounting
Best for: Agencies already using TrustSoft for trust accounting, or those who need both trust accounting and AML compliance in one place. For agencies that only need AML compliance, the trust accounting overhead may not be worth it at this price point.
AML Assured
amlassured.com — newer Australian RE-specific platform
Pricing TBC
Not yet published — early-bird pricing for first 100 agencies. Waitlist model.

AML Assured is a newer Australian-built tool focused exclusively on real estate. Its pitch is automated workflows, one-click audit reports, and a compliance trail attached to each property record. It's positioning itself between SimpleAML (free, basic) and First AML (full-featured, expensive). Pricing hasn't been published at time of writing, and it was still in early-bird / waitlist mode as of March 2026.

What it looks like it does well
  • RE-specific — built for property workflows
  • Australian-built and focused
  • Per-property compliance trail is a smart design
  • Automated workflows reduce manual work
Limitations / unknowns
  • Pricing not published — hard to evaluate
  • Newer product — less proven track record
  • Still in early-bird phase as of March 2026
  • Feature depth unverified at time of writing
Best for: Agencies who want a purpose-built Australian RE tool with more automation than SimpleAML, but aren't ready to commit to First AML's pricing. Worth watching — but with pricing unknown, it's hard to recommend over proven alternatives right now.

Which tool is right for your agency?

SimpleAML

Small principal-led agency

1–5 agents, under 50 transactions/month, want to be compliant without spending a dollar. You handle ID verification manually or via a cheap standalone service. SimpleAML gives you everything you legally need.

First AML

Mid-large agency or franchise

10+ agents, high transaction volume, dedicated compliance staff, or franchise group needing a consistent system across multiple offices. Budget for $5k+ pa and expect a proper onboarding process.

TrustSoft

Already using TrustSoft

You're an existing TrustSoft user for trust accounting, or you need both trust accounting and AML compliance in one platform. The $1,200 pa AML module addition makes good sense in this context.

AML Assured

Wait and see

Looks promising for mid-sized agencies wanting an affordable automated option. Hold off until pricing is published and the platform has more of a track record — then reassess.

Can I use SimpleAML and then upgrade later?

Yes. SimpleAML is designed to get you compliant by 1 July 2026 with zero cost and zero friction. If your agency grows, your transaction volume increases, or you want automated ID verification workflows, you can migrate to a paid tool later.

Your compliance records export from SimpleAML as JSON, so your data isn't locked in. The Word templates you download are yours to keep regardless of which tool you ultimately use.

For the majority of small agencies in Australia — and there are thousands of them — SimpleAML is sufficient to meet the legal requirements. You don't need enterprise software to be compliant. You need a risk assessment, a written program, trained staff, and a CDD record for each listing. SimpleAML helps you do all of that, for free, today.

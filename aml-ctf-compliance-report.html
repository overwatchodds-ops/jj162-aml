AML/CTF
Compliance Report

AUSTRAC can request a Compliance Report from any reporting entity at any time. SimpleAML generates a summary of your firm's compliance position across all eight areas — ready when you need it.

The Requirement

What is AUSTRAC's Compliance Report?

AUSTRAC has the power to request a Compliance Report from any reporting entity. The report requires you to summarise your firm's AML/CTF compliance position — covering your program, risk assessment, CDD practices, training, staff vetting, and reporting activity. For most small practices, this will be an annual obligation once the Tranche 2 framework is fully in force.

The Compliance Report is submitted directly to AUSTRAC. It is not a document you prepare in advance and file — it is a response to a request, drawing on the records you should already have in place.

The report is only as good as your underlying records. AUSTRAC's Compliance Report asks you to describe what you did — how many clients you onboarded, how many were screened, whether training was conducted, whether any SMRs were filed. If your records are incomplete, your Compliance Report will reflect that.

What It Covers

What does the report need to address?

A complete Compliance Report covers every aspect of your AML/CTF program. The eight areas AUSTRAC expects you to be able to report on are:

  • Firm profile — practice details and compliance appointments, including your AMLCO
  • AML/CTF Risk Assessment — designated services, inherent risk ratings, and risk appetite
  • AML/CTF Program — documentation, senior manager approval, and review history
  • AUSTRAC Enrolment — controls declared, residual risk, and enrolment details
  • Key Personnel Vetting — staff assessed, classifications, and fit and proper check status
  • Training Register — AML/CTF training records for all staff performing AML/CTF functions
  • Client Register — CDD status, entity types, and new, ongoing, and dormant client summary
  • SMR & Incident Register — suspicious matter reports and compliance incidents recorded

The SimpleAML Compliance Report is not submitted to AUSTRAC. It is a summary of your compliance records to help you prepare when AUSTRAC requests a Compliance Report. The actual submission is made directly through the AUSTRAC portal. Generating this report regularly is good practice — it shows you are actively monitoring your compliance position.

How SimpleAML Handles It

How SimpleAML generates your Compliance Report

SimpleAML is a compliance register, not a document storage system. The Compliance Report summarises what your register contains — it draws on the records you have already built across all eight sections of the app. There is nothing to upload or attach. The report reflects what you have recorded.

What the app does for you

  • One-click PDF generation — produces a summary of your firm's compliance position across all eight sections from the records already in SimpleAML
  • Covers all eight required areas — firm profile, risk assessment, program, enrolment, staff vetting, training, client register, and SMR register
  • Storage location field — record where you have saved the downloaded PDF (e.g. SharePoint, Google Drive) so the location is part of your compliance trail
  • Generation history log — a permanent record of every time the report was generated and where it was stored, with the ability to remove individual entries
  • 7-year retention reminder — the report must be retained as a compliance record under the AML/CTF Act
  • Live dashboard — your compliance status across all eight areas is always visible without needing to generate a report, so you can see and resolve gaps in real time

By generating this report you confirm that you have sighted all underlying evidence documents and stored copies in your firm's own records. SimpleAML records the compliance trail — your identity documents, program files, training certificates, and screening reports stay in your cabinet, where they belong.

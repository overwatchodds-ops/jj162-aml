Getting Started

Where do I start? What order should I complete things in?
How-to

We recommend following this order when setting up SimpleAML for the first time:

  1. Firm Profile — enter your practice details, ABN and principal contact
  2. Services — tick which designated services your firm provides. This is the foundation of everything else
  3. Appointments — record who holds each compliance role (AMLCO, Reporting Officer etc). Sole practitioners can fill all roles at once
  4. Client Base & Geography — complete your Firm Risk Assessment including service risk, client base risk and PF risk
  5. Policy & Approval — upload your AML/CTF Program document and record senior manager approval
  6. Training Register — log AML/CTF training completed by staff
  7. AUSTRAC Enrolment — check your readiness score and enrol once all requirements are met
  8. Client Risk Profiler — add clients and complete CDD for each one before providing a designated service
The AUSTRAC Enrolment tab shows a live readiness indicator — it tells you exactly what's still missing before you can enrol.
Can I use SimpleAML on my phone or tablet?
How-to

Yes — SimpleAML works in any modern browser on any device. However for the best experience we recommend using it on a desktop or laptop computer. The app has a lot of detail and some sections are easier to work through on a larger screen.

The website (simpleaml.com.au) is fully mobile-friendly. The app itself is optimised for tablet and desktop.

Do I need to create an account?
How-to

No account is required to use SimpleAML. Just open the app and enter your firm name to get started. Your data is saved automatically to your browser and backed up to our secure cloud.

User accounts with login are on our roadmap — this will allow multiple staff members to access the same firm data from different devices. See the Multiple Users section for more detail.

Using the App

Why can't everything be a dropdown or tick box?
Compliance

This is one of the most common questions we get — and it's a fair one. The short answer is that AUSTRAC's risk-based framework requires your own written assessment for key areas, not just a checkbox saying you did it.

A tick box shows you completed a step. A written narrative shows you actually understood it and applied it to your specific firm. During an AUSTRAC audit, you'll be asked to explain and defend your assessments — your written narrative is the evidence.

The areas that specifically require written answers include:

  • Firm risk assessment — why you rated your services Low/Medium/High
  • Risk appetite statement — what client types and services your firm will and won't accept
  • Proliferation financing assessment — your specific PF exposure and controls
  • Source of funds (for High Risk clients) — a specific documented explanation
  • Key controls — what your practice actually does, not a generic list
SimpleAML uses dropdowns and tick boxes where AUSTRAC allows it, and guided text fields where written narrative is required. The design is intentional.
How do I add a new client?
How-to

Go to Client Risk Profiler in the sidebar. Fill in the client's details including entity type, designated service, jurisdiction and the nature of the relationship. Add at least one individual for identity screening, then set a risk rating and review date. Click Record Compliance Profile to save.

CDD must be completed before you provide a designated service to a client — not after. SimpleAML is designed to be used at onboarding, not as a retrospective record.

What is a risk rating and how is it calculated?
Compliance

Each client is assigned a risk rating of Low, Medium or High based on factors including their industry, jurisdiction, the designated service being provided, and whether any suspicious matter or threshold transaction triggers apply.

You can set the risk rating manually or let the app calculate it automatically from the information you enter. The rating determines how often a client review is required:

  • High Risk — annual review
  • Medium Risk — every 2 years
  • Low Risk — every 3 years

High Risk clients also require enhanced CDD — source of funds documentation, associates screening, and a written reason for proceeding.

What happens when a client review is due?
How-to

When a client's review date passes, they are flagged as Overdue in red on the Dashboard and Client Overview. The Dashboard also shows how many reviews are due in the next 30 days so you can plan ahead.

To complete a review, go to Client Overview, find the client and click New Review. The form will pre-fill with the client's existing details — update anything that has changed and save. The previous record is retained as a superseded version for your audit trail.

What documents do I need to upload for each client?
Compliance

For each individual associated with a client you should upload evidence of:

  • DVS Identity check — Document Verification Service result confirming their identity
  • PEP screening — Politically Exposed Person check result
  • Sanctions screening — confirmation they are not on the DFAT consolidated sanctions list
  • Associates screening — optional for Low Risk, recommended for Medium, required for High Risk

For High Risk clients you also need source of funds documentation — bank statements, payslips, or other evidence of where their funds come from.

SimpleAML recommends NameScan for DVS, PEP, sanctions and associates screening in one place.
Who does Personnel Due Diligence apply to?
Compliance

AUSTRAC uses the term "personnel" broadly — it's not limited to permanent employees. It covers anyone involved in your AML/CTF program, including employees, contractors, consultants and partners.

For most small accounting firms this means all staff who have contact with clients or handle any compliance-related tasks. If in doubt, include them — it's better to document more than less.

Personnel Due Diligence must be completed before appointment for anyone in a compliance role, and reviewed annually as part of ongoing monitoring.

Can I save or print my compliance records?
How-to

Yes — use the Save as PDF button in the Tools section of the sidebar. This opens your browser's print dialog where you can select "Save as PDF" to save the current tab as a PDF file.

Note that this saves only the currently visible tab, not the entire app. A full one-click compliance report that exports all sections into a single PDF is on our roadmap.

You can also use Export Data to download a complete backup of all your data as a JSON file, which can be imported back into SimpleAML on any device.

What is the Export Report for?
Compliance

The Export Report button generates a formal AML/CTF Compliance Evidence Report specifically formatted for AUSTRAC audit purposes. It is the single most important document you can produce to prove your firm is compliant.

The report documents everything AUSTRAC expects to see during an audit:

  • Your AUSTRAC enrolment details and entity ID
  • Your AML/CTF Program — Part A governance and Part B CDD procedures
  • Your ML/TF/PF Risk Assessment with written narrative
  • Your designated services scope and risk appetite statement
  • Your compliance appointments including AMLCO
  • Your complete client CDD register
  • Your staff training register
  • Your personnel due diligence records

Each section references the specific AML/CTF Act provision it satisfies. The report includes a formal declaration and signature block for your AMLCO and senior manager.

Save the report as PDF and keep it on file. Update it whenever your compliance records change — before and after client reviews, after training, and after any program updates. If AUSTRAC contacts you, this document is your first line of evidence.

AML/CTF Compliance

What is ML, TF and PF?
Compliance

ML — Money Laundering. The process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate. For example, a client using your trust account to layer funds from an undisclosed or illegal source.

TF — Terrorism Financing. Providing funds or financial services — knowingly or unknowingly — that support terrorist activities. Even small, legitimate-looking transactions can be used to finance terrorism.

PF — Proliferation Financing. Funding the development, production or acquisition of weapons of mass destruction — nuclear, chemical, biological or radiological weapons. This is a newer requirement added to the reformed AML/CTF Act.

When SimpleAML refers to ML/TF/PF risk it means: what is the risk that your firm's services could be used — knowingly or unknowingly — to launder money, finance terrorism, or finance weapons proliferation. Your Firm Risk Assessment documents your assessment of this risk across all three categories.

What is the difference between the PF Statement and the PF Risk Assessment?
Compliance

They serve different purposes and appear in different parts of the app.

PF Statement (Services tab) is your firm's policy declaration — a written statement of your firm's position on proliferation financing. It's forward-looking and says "here is what our firm will and won't do." For example: "Our firm will not provide services to any entity subject to UN Security Council sanctions. All clients are screened against the DFAT consolidated list on onboarding."

PF Risk Assessment (Client Base & Geography tab) is your evidence-based analysis — a documented assessment of your actual exposure to PF risk based on your real client base, services and jurisdictions. It requires a rating (Low/Medium/High) and written justification. For example: "Our firm's PF risk is rated Low because our client base consists entirely of Australian SMEs with no exposure to high-risk jurisdictions or defence-related industries."

Think of it this way: the Statement is your policy, the Assessment is your evidence. They should reference each other but not duplicate each other.
What are designated services?
Compliance

Designated services are the specific accounting services that trigger AML/CTF obligations under Tranche 2. Your obligations apply based on what services you provide, not simply because you are an accountant.

The nine designated services for accountants under Tranche 2 are listed in the Services tab of the app. Common examples include assisting with real estate transactions, receiving or managing client funds, creating or restructuring companies or trusts, acting as a nominee, and selling shelf companies.

A common misconception is "we don't handle cash, so AML doesn't apply." Many firms are in scope due to trust accounts, corporate restructures, or acting as a nominee — even without touching physical cash.

If you are unsure whether a service applies to your firm, tick it. It is better to document more than less. Seek legal advice if you are uncertain about your scope.
What is an AMLCO?
Compliance

AMLCO stands for AML/CTF Compliance Officer. Every reporting entity must appoint an AMLCO before enrolling with AUSTRAC. The AMLCO is responsible for:

  • Overseeing the firm's AML/CTF program
  • Ensuring compliance with AUSTRAC requirements
  • Acting as the primary regulatory liaison with AUSTRAC
  • Reporting suspicious matters and threshold transactions

The AMLCO must meet AUSTRAC's fit and proper person requirements — they must be of good character, have relevant knowledge and skills, and not have been convicted of relevant offences.

For sole practitioners, you will typically be your own AMLCO. SimpleAML's Appointments tab includes a "Fill All Roles" shortcut for this purpose.

When do I need to enrol with AUSTRAC?
Compliance

Key dates for Tranche 2 enrolment:

  • 31 March 2026 — Enrolment opens
  • 1 July 2026 — AML/CTF obligations commence
  • 29 July 2026 — Enrolment deadline for entities providing designated services from 1 July 2026

You must complete all six requirements before enrolling — designated services confirmed, firm risk assessment complete, AML/CTF Program uploaded and approved, AMLCO appointed, and staff training on file. The AUSTRAC Enrolment tab in SimpleAML shows your readiness score and tells you exactly what's still missing.

Your Data

Where is my data stored?
Data

Your data is stored in two places simultaneously:

  • Your browser — saved locally in your browser's storage. This means your data is available instantly without an internet connection.
  • Our secure cloud — automatically backed up to Google Firebase (Firestore) each time you save. This protects against data loss if you clear your browser.

We do not sell your data or share it with third parties. Your compliance records are yours.

What happens if I clear my browser or switch browsers?
Data

If you clear your browser's local storage your data will appear to be gone — but don't panic. Because SimpleAML also backs up to our cloud, your data can be restored automatically when you reopen the app in the same browser on the same device.

If you switch to a different browser on the same device, the new browser won't have your local data. The app will appear empty. This is because each browser has its own separate local storage.

We strongly recommend using Export Data regularly (Tools section in the sidebar) to download a backup file. Store this somewhere safe — such as your practice's file server or cloud drive.
How do I move my data to a different device?
Data

Currently SimpleAML is tied to a device — your data on one device won't automatically appear on another device. To move your data:

  1. On your original device — go to Tools → Export Data in the sidebar
  2. Save the downloaded JSON file somewhere accessible (email it to yourself, save to a USB, etc)
  3. On the new device — open SimpleAML, go to Tools → Import Data
  4. Select the JSON file — your data will be fully restored
User accounts with login are on our roadmap. Once available, you'll be able to log in from any device and your data will sync automatically.
How long do I need to keep my AML/CTF records?
Compliance

Under the AML/CTF Act, reporting entities are required to retain all AML/CTF records for a minimum of 7 years from the date the record was made or the transaction was completed.

This includes customer identification records, transaction records, and AML/CTF program documentation. Failure to retain records for the required period may result in regulatory action by AUSTRAC.

SimpleAML stores your records in the cloud, but you are ultimately responsible for maintaining your own records. We strongly recommend regular data exports and storing backups in your practice's document management system.

Multiple Users

Can two people use SimpleAML at the same time?
Account

Currently SimpleAML is designed for one person managing compliance on one device. If two people open the app on different devices they will each see different data — there is no automatic syncing between devices.

The best current workaround for small teams is to nominate one device as the "compliance computer" where SimpleAML lives, and have one person responsible for keeping it up to date. Others can view a saved PDF export.

Multi-user access with role-based permissions is our top development priority. It will allow multiple staff to log in from any device and access the same firm data with appropriate access levels.
We're an office — how can our team share access?
Account

For an office team right now the most practical approach is:

  • Use one designated computer and browser as the primary SimpleAML device
  • Export your data regularly and share the backup file with team members who need a copy
  • Anyone who needs to add clients can do so on the designated device

We know this isn't ideal. The upcoming user accounts feature will solve this properly — the AMLCO will have full access, admin staff can add clients and log training, and principals can view and approve — all from their own devices.

Will user accounts be available?
Account

Yes — user accounts with login are our top development priority. The planned system will allow:

  • One firm account tied to an email address, not a device
  • Multiple users — each with their own login
  • Role-based access — AMLCO (full access), Principal (read and approve), Admin (clients and training), Junior (clients only)
  • Any device — log in from any browser, anywhere

This will also resolve the different-device problem entirely. We'll notify existing users when this feature is available.
